⚡ Sprint Mode — how to use it
  • Core lessons are highlighted with a teal left bar and a Core badge. Do these first — they cover what interviewers actually test.
  • Quiz yourself, don’t just re-read — retrieve the answer before looking it up. That’s what builds durable memory, not passive review.
  • Break every 45 minutes — rest means away from screens, not just away from this tab. Scrolling, YouTube, or group chats use the same cognitive systems you just trained. A short walk or staring out a window lets your brain replay and compress what you studied.
  • Move on when you can self-quiz from memory — “good enough now” beats “perfect too late.” You can come back after the sprint.

Read It Aloud — how every lesson works

Every formula is broken down argument by argument into plain language. Once you can say it out loud — in any language — you own it.

=VLOOKUP(A2, products!A:D, 3, FALSE)
A2→the value we're searching for
products!A:D→search in this table (first column gets searched)
3→return the value from the 3rd column of that range
FALSE→exact match only — no close guesses
